Understanding the Impact of Global Warming

Join us in taking action to protect our planet for future generations.

What Causes Global Warming?

Global warming is primarily caused by the excessive release of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide. Carbon dioxide is released through the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas for energy and transportation, while deforestation further contributes by reducing the Earth's capacity to absorb carbon dioxide. Methane, another potent greenhouse gas, is emitted during agricultural activities, particularly from livestock digestion, and from the decay of organic waste in landfills. Nitrous oxide is generated from industrial and agricultural activities, especially through the extensive use of synthetic fertilizers.

The Effects of Global Warming

The impacts of global warming are far-reaching and profoundly alarming, affecting every aspect of life on Earth. Rising sea levels, driven by the melting of polar ice caps and glaciers, threaten coastal communities and habitats, displacing millions of people and wildlife. Extreme weather events, such as hurricanes, droughts, and floods, are becoming more frequent and intense, causing widespread destruction and loss of life. Global warming also disrupts ecosystems, endangering biodiversity as species struggle to adapt to rapidly changing climates. Forests are succumbing to wildfires, coral reefs are bleaching, and vital habitats are disappearing, all while weather patterns grow increasingly erratic and unpredictable
